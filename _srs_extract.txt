Introduction
Purpose
The purpose of this Software Requirements Specification (SRS) is to describe the functional and non-functional requirements for the Undergraduate Transfer Management System (UTMS) for İzmir Institute of Technology (IZTECH). This document is intended for the development team, project stakeholders (Student Affairs, Dean’s Office, Transfer Commission, YDYO), and the client to ensure a shared understanding of the system.
Scope
The UTMS is a web-based platform designed to automate and streamline the horizontal transfer (yatay geçiş) process. The scope includes:
All system users (Applicant, Transfer Commission, Student Affairs, Dean’s Office, YDYO) can securely log in with email & password, supporting password validation and session management.
Users can reset forgotten passwords
Online application submission and document upload for students.
Applicants can view the real-time status, stages, and results of their transfer applications.
Automated data retrieval from UBYS, ÖSYM, and YÖKSİS.
Automated eligibility checks (GPA, Exam Scores) and rule-based validation.
Approval of document verification for Student Affairs
Check and manage the verified documents if needed for Student Affairs
Students can ask their questions to Students Affairs and students affairs can reply to them
Notification of the status of the student’s application
Registration and assignment of the roles to the staff for IT
Integration of the application date and deadline into the system for IT
Approvement of English proficiency for YDYO
Announce the English proficiency exam results for YDYO
Conversion of GPA for Transfer Commision
Verifying the yks scores for Transfer Commision
Ability to check the department specific requirements and approving the ranking generated by the system for Transfer Commision
Digital routing of applications between Student Affairs, Dean’s Offices, and Departments.
Semi-automated course equivalency (Intibak) mapping interface.
Automated scoring, ranking, and result announcement with notifications.

User Characteristics
Applicant (Student): University students capable of using web interfaces; familiar with basic academic terminology, able to understand English.
Student Affairs Staff: Administrative personnel; require efficient tools for batch processing, document checks notifying results.
Faculty Dean’s Office: Academic administrators responsible for approving, forwarding, and finalizing transfer-related decisions.
Transfer Commission (Academic Staff): Faculty members needing detailed transcript views, GPA/YKS data, and tools for course equivalency (intibak) evaluation.
YDYO (School of Foreign Languages) Staff: Personnel responsible for English proficiency validation, requiring interfaces for exam result entry and student status approval.
IT Department Staff: Technical staff responsible for role assignment, configuration, system dates, and maintaining platform operability.
Constraints
 Regulatory Compliance:The system must comply with YÖK regulations (e.g., 4.0 GPA scale conversion), ÖSYM policies, and University Senate rules.
 Deadline Dependency:The application process is strictly time sensitive. System downtime during the application window is unacceptable.
 File Format Restrictions:Uploaded documents must follow predefined formats (PDF only) to ensure readability and secure processing.
 Identity Verification Constraint:System must rely on external identity services (e-Devlet, YÖKSİS). Any downtime in these services directly affects registration and data validation.
 Data Integrity Constraint:Automated eligibility and ranking calculations must follow deterministic, non-manipulable rules defined by academic regulations.
 Security & Privacy Constraint:The system must operate over encrypted channels (HTTPS) and comply with KVKK / GDPR-like standards for storing personal data.
 Role-Based Access Constraint:Only authorized university staff may access applicant data, perform approvals, or view sensitive documents.
 Performance Constraint:Login, document validation, and status retrieval operations must return results within acceptable time limits (<5 seconds for login, <2 min latency for status updates with 5mbps internet connection).
 System Integration Constraint:The system depends on real-time or scheduled integrations with UBYS, EBYS, YÖKSİS, and ÖSYM. Their schema and API limitations constrain UTMS functionality.
Assumptions and Dependencies
Internet Connectivity: All users are assumed to have a stable internet connection with a minimum speed of 5 Mbps, required for login, document uploads, and accessing real-time application status.
External API Availability: The system depends on uninterrupted access to YÖKSİS, ÖSYM, e-Devlet, and UBYS services. These third-party APIs are assumed to be operational, reachable, and within their normal rate limits during the application period.
Identity: Applicants possess a valid National ID.
Compliance With Deadlines: It is assumed that all academic units (Student Affairs, YDYO, Dean’s Office, Commission) will perform reviews within the official university deadlines configured in the system.
Email Service Reliability: The university SMTP/Email service is assumed to be operational for password resets, notifications.
Apportioning of Requirements
Must-Have (Release 1 – Critical Requirements)
These features are essential for the transfer process to function and must be delivered in the first release:
Applicant registration, login, document upload, and application submission
Real-time status tracking and viewing results
Automatic document validation, GPA conversion, eligibility checks, and ranking
Administrative verification tools (Student Affairs, Dean’s Office, Commission)
Final decision support and publication of results
Integration with external systems (YÖKSİS, ÖSYM, e-Devlet, UBYS)
Mandatory constraints: role-based access, HTTPS security, PDF-only uploads, deterministic scoring rules, performance limits
2. Should-Have (Release 2 – Important but Not Critical)
These improve administrative efficiency but are not required for the core transfer flow:
Staff role assignment and configuration features
Batch processing tools
YDYO module for proficiency result entry
Course equivalency (intibak) preparation tools
Additional notification types (password reset, clarification messages)
3. Could-Have (Future Releases – Enhancements)
These features enhance usability and long-term maintainability:
In-system Q&A messaging for applicants
Analytics dashboards and exportable reports
Automated reminders and extended configuration options
Performance optimizations for large applicant pools

Special Requirements:
SR1. Authentication Protocol RequirementThe system shall authenticate all actors through OAuth 2.0 Authorization Code Flow.
SR2. Secure Transmission RequirementAll login credentials shall be transmitted via HTTPS secured with TLS 1.2 or higher.
SR3. Password Storage RequirementPasswords shall be stored only in hashed and salted form using a cryptographic one-way hashing algorithm (e.g., bcrypt/Argon2).
SR4. Session Security RequirementAfter successful login, the system shall issue a Secure, HttpOnly session token to prevent client-side access or script injection.
SR5. Account Protection RequirementAfter 5 failed login attempts, the system shall temporarily lock the account and prevent additional attempts for a defined security timeout period.
SR6. Privacy RequirementError messages shall be generic and shall not reveal whether the entered email exists in the system.
Assumptions:

User Interface
Performance/Software quality requirements
 The system shall process 95% of all login requests within 5 seconds under normal load.
 The system shall display incorrect credential error messages within 2 seconds.
 The authentication subsystem shall support at least 1,000 concurrent login requests without degradation.
 During peak load (e.g., start of transfer period), the system shall handle up to 5,000 login attempts per minute with a maximum response time of 8 seconds.
 The login service shall maintain at least 99.5% availability during the transfer period.
 The system shall operate correctly for users with a minimum stable internet connection of 5 Mbps.
 The system shall ensure that authentication logs are written and stored without causing delays in login processing.
 The backend infrastructure shall scale automatically or elastically during periods of increased traffic to maintain required response times.
 The system shall ensure that session creation and token issuance do not exceed 1 second of processing time.
 The system shall be able to recover from a partial service interruption and resume authentication functionality within 30 seconds after restoration.

2. Database ACID Compliance Requirement
The UTMS database shall adhere to ACID properties to guarantee the reliability and consistency of all transactions:
Atomicity:Each transaction must be processed as an indivisible unit. Partial updates are not allowed; operations either fully complete or are fully rolled back.
Consistency:All transactions must transition the database from one valid state to another, ensuring that constraints, rules, and validation policies are preserved.
Isolation:Concurrent operations must not interfere with each other. The system shall ensure that simultaneous transactions execute as if they were executed sequentially.
Durability:Once a transaction is committed, its results must be permanently stored and remain intact even in the event of system crashes or failures.
These ACID standards ensure integrity for critical operations such as identity verification, application submission, document handling, ranking generation, and inter-departmental processing.
